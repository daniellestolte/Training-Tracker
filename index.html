🌈 Your cycle & training

🔴 Menstrual phase — Days 1–5
Energy and strength are at their lowest. Oestrogen and progesterone are both low. This is not a phase to push — it is a phase to honour.

Training: Keep runs easy and conversational. Skip or reduce strength if cramping. Yoga, walking, and gentle stretching are ideal. Your pain tolerance is also lower, so don't be hard on yourself if everything feels harder.
Flexibility: Actually a great time for gentle stretching — the body is more relaxed.
Nutrition: Iron-rich foods (red meat, lentils, dark leafy greens). Magnesium for cramps. Stay warm. More sleep.
🌿 Follicular phase — Days 6–13
Rising oestrogen means rising energy, mood, and motivation. This is your feel-good, do-everything phase. Your body responds best to new challenges here.

Training: Best phase for tempo runs, interval sessions, and heavy strength work. Your body recovers faster now. Try your hardest sessions in this window. Sand sprints on Saturday will feel electric.
Flexibility: Muscles are more pliable — great for splits progress.
Nutrition: Higher carb days support the energy you have. Cruciferous veg (broccoli, kale) help oestrogen metabolism.
☀️ Ovulation — Days 14–17
Peak oestrogen and a testosterone surge. You are strongest, fastest, and most coordinated right now. This is your superpower window.

Training: Go for it. Long runs, PB attempts on pace, heavy lifts. Your grip strength, power output, and pain tolerance are all at their highest. If a big RunKeeper session falls here, race it.
Caution: Oestrogen also loosens ligaments slightly around ovulation — warm up properly and don't do anything reckless with your joints.
Nutrition: Anti-inflammatory foods. Fibre-rich to support oestrogen clearance.
🌙 Luteal phase — Days 18–35
Progesterone rises, temperature increases slightly, and energy gradually drops toward period. Early luteal (days 18–24) is still strong. Late luteal (days 25+) is where PMS symptoms may appear.

Training: Shift toward steady, moderate effort. Relaxed runs feel better than intervals. Strength work is still effective but you may need longer rest between sets. Listen to your body — fatigue and mood changes are hormonal, not weakness.
Flexibility: Progesterone can cause some water retention — don't be discouraged if splits feel tighter.
Nutrition: Complex carbs reduce cravings. Magnesium and B6 for mood. More protein for satiety. Reduce salt to minimise bloating.

Pre-run warm-up (5 min) 🏃️‍♀️

Always dynamic before a run — never static holds. Gets blood into the joints before impact.
Leg swings
Stand next to a wall. 10 swings forward/back each leg, then 10 side to side. Swing from the hip socket, not the knee.
Form tip: keep your planted leg soft (not locked), core lightly engaged throughout
Hip circles
Hands on hips, draw big slow circles with your hips. 10 each direction per leg. Think hula hoop — really exaggerate the movement.
Form tip: initiate the circle from deep in the hip socket, not just moving your knees
Walking lunges with a twist
10 walking lunge steps. As you lunge, rotate your torso toward your front knee. Alternate sides with each step. Opens hips and fires up core simultaneously.
Form tip: front knee stays directly over your ankle — never letting it cave inward
High knees (slow and deliberate)
20 reps, lifting each knee toward hip height. This is about muscle activation, not cardio — slow and controlled, not a sprint.
Form tip: drive through the ball of your standing foot on each rep, not the heel
Ankle circles + calf raises
10 full slow circles each ankle, then 15 calf raises both feet. Preps the achilles tendon and ankle joint for repetitive ground impact.
Glute kickbacks
15 each side standing (hands on wall) or on all fours. Squeeze the glute hard at the top. Fires up the engine before your run does.
Form tip: keep hips square — don't let them rotate open as you kick

Post-run stretch (10–15 min) 🧦️‍♀️

Do this within 10 minutes of finishing while still warm. On Wed and Sat, go straight into splits work right after.
Standing quad stretch
Hold ankle, pull heel toward glute, stand tall. 45 sec each side. Use a wall or tree for balance. Keep your hips level — don't let the stretched side hike up.
Form tip: squeeze the glute of the stretched leg and tuck the pelvis slightly to deepen the hip flexor component
Standing calf stretch
Hands on a wall, straight leg back with heel flat on the ground. 45 sec. Then bend that back knee for another 30 sec to target the soleus (deeper calf muscle). Each side.
Lying hamstring stretch
On your back, loop a towel or resistance band around one foot. Gently straighten leg toward the sky. Other leg stays flat on the ground. 60 sec each side.
Form tip: don't force the leg straight — let it find its range without the knee locking uncomfortably
Hip flexor lunge stretch
Low lunge, back knee on the ground. Sink hips forward and down. 60 sec each side. This is the single most important stretch for runners — tight hip flexors cause lower back pain and shorten stride length.
Form tip: tuck the tailbone under (posterior pelvic tilt) to target the hip flexor rather than just the quad
Figure-4 glute stretch
Lying on back, cross one ankle over opposite knee. Pull the uncrossed leg toward your chest. 60 sec each side. Targets the piriformis muscle which often gets tight in runners.
Child's pose
Knees wide, sit hips toward heels, arms extended forward. 90 sec. Decompresses the spine and stretches the lower back after the impact of running.

Core circuit A — runner stability (Tuesday) 🌀

Dead bug
Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90 degrees. Slowly lower opposite arm and leg toward the floor, return. 3 x 10 each side. Your lower back MUST stay flat against the floor the entire time — if it lifts, you've gone too far.
Form tip: exhale as you lower the limbs. Breath helps keep the core braced and the back down
Hollow body hold
Lie on back, press lower back flat, lift arms overhead and legs to about 45 degrees. Hold 20–30 sec for 3 rounds. You should look like a shallow banana. Builds the deep core that holds your posture in the last 2km of a race.
Form tip: if your lower back lifts, raise your legs higher until it stays flat
Plank with shoulder taps
In a strong plank, tap opposite shoulder alternately. 3 x 20 taps. The challenge is keeping your hips perfectly still as you lift each hand. This is anti-rotation core work — exactly what running demands.
Form tip: widen your feet for stability. No hip sway allowed
Side plank
On forearm, body in one straight diagonal line, hips lifted. 3 x 30 sec each side. Builds the lateral hip stability that protects your knees with every stride.
Form tip: don't let the bottom hip sag. Stack your feet or stagger them for more stability
Glute bridge march
In a glute bridge (hips raised), lift one foot slightly, hold 2 sec, switch. 3 x 10 each leg. Challenges glute and core to stabilise together — exactly what happens in each stride when you're on one leg.

Core circuit B — strength (Thursday) 🔥

Bicycle crunches
Hands behind head, rotate elbow to opposite knee while extending the other leg. 3 x 20 reps, SLOW. Slower = more effective. The rotation comes from your ribcage, not your neck.
Form tip: keep your lower back pressed to the floor — if it arches, you've lost the contraction
Leg raises
Lying on back, legs straight (or slightly bent). Lower from 90 degrees slowly to just above the floor. 3 x 12. The lowering phase is where the work happens — go as slow as you can. If your back lifts, bend your knees more.
Russian twists
Seated, leaning back at about 45 degrees, feet lifted or on the floor. Rotate side to side touching the ground each time. 3 x 20. Hold a water bottle for extra resistance. Keep the chest tall throughout.
Mountain climbers
In a solid plank, drive knees to chest alternately at a controlled pace. 3 x 30 sec. Don't let the hips rise. Core endurance plus cardio in one exercise.
Form tip: find the pace where you can still breathe through your nose — that's your working pace
Superman hold
Face down, arms extended overhead. Lift arms, chest and legs simultaneously. Hold 2 sec at the top, lower slowly. 3 x 12. Lower back strength — the forgotten component of a strong core.

Upper body (Thursday) 💪

Push-up (follow your progression)
3 sets at current level from the push-up tracker. Rest 60–90 sec between sets. Every variation counts — progress is progress.
Form tip for all push-up variations: hands slightly wider than shoulders, body completely straight, elbows track back at 45 degrees — not flared out sideways
Resistance band rows
Anchor band to a tree, fence, or post at chest height. Stand back until there's tension. Pull both elbows back simultaneously, squeezing shoulder blades together. 3 x 15. Builds the back strength that makes push-ups significantly easier.
Form tip: shoulders stay down away from your ears throughout the movement — no shrugging
Band pull-aparts
Hold band at chest height, arms extended forward. Pull apart to a T shape, squeezing shoulder blades at the end. 3 x 20. Excellent for posture and rear shoulder strength.
Pike push-ups
Start in a downward dog (hips high, V-shape). Bend elbows to lower top of head toward the floor, push back up. 3 x 8–10. Heavy shoulder targeting. Bend knees to make it more manageable.
Tricep dips
Hands on a bench, chair, or low wall behind you. Lower by bending elbows to 90 degrees, press back up. 3 x 10. Critical for the push-up lockout — the top portion where triceps do most of the work.
Form tip: keep your back close to the surface, elbows pointing straight back not out to the sides

Strength A — lower body (Tuesday) 🦶

Single-leg glute bridges
Lie on back, one foot flat on the floor, other leg extended straight up. Drive through the planted heel to lift hips. 3 x 15 each side. The most important exercise for preventing running knee injuries.
Form tip: squeeze the glute hard at the top and hold 1 sec. Hips stay level — not twisted to one side
Reverse lunges
Step one foot back, lower back knee toward the floor, push off the front foot to return. 3 x 12 each leg. Safer for knees than forward lunges. Add weight with rocks or a backpack.
Form tip: front knee stays over your ankle — if it's going past your toes, widen your stance
Lateral band walks
Band around ankles (or just above knees). Walk sideways 20 steps each direction. Keep a slight squat throughout. Your hip abductors are what keep knees tracking straight when running.
Form tip: don't let feet come completely together between steps — keep constant tension on the band
Single-leg calf raises
Stand on one foot, lower heel below a step edge, then rise up on the ball of your foot. 3 x 20 each side. Builds achilles tendon resilience which becomes critical as running volume increases.
Form tip: the lowering (eccentric) phase is where most adaptation happens — go slow on the way down
Step-ups
Step one foot onto a bench, rock, or low wall. Drive through that heel to bring your whole body up. Lower under control. 3 x 12 each leg. Directly mirrors the single-leg loading that happens in every running stride.

Strength B — beach session (Saturday) 🏈️

Sand sprints (15–20m)
6–8 reps. Flat out effort for 15–20m. Walk slowly back, fully recover (1–2 min), then go again. Sand massively increases resistance and reduces joint impact. Go barefoot on firm sand.
Form tip: lean forward slightly from the ankles (not the waist), drive your knees up high, pump your arms hard and fast
Jump squats
Squat to about 90 degrees, then explode upward. Land softly with bent knees, absorbing the impact. 3 x 12 on sand. The soft surface makes landings more forgiving while the push-off is harder.
Push-up variation (extra practice)
2 sets at current level. A second weekly push-up session is one of the fastest ways to accelerate progress — frequency is more important than volume when you're building a new movement pattern.
Bear crawls
Knees hovering just above the sand, crawl forward 20m. Keep hips LOW — resist the urge to stick your bum in the air. Full body: core, shoulders, hips, wrists, coordination. Sand makes this dramatically harder than it looks.
Resistance band rows (anchored)
Anchor to a post or tree. 3 x 15 with same technique as Thursday. Second back strength session per week — upper body balance for running posture.

Splits routine (20 min) — warm muscles only 🤺️

Always after running or a warm-up. Cold stretching does almost nothing. Warm muscle is pliable muscle.
Low lunge hold
Step into a lunge, back knee on the ground. Sink hips forward and down. Feel the stretch through the front of your back hip. 2 min each side. This is the primary exercise for front splits.
Form tip: tuck your tailbone under and gently push the back hip toward the floor throughout
Pigeon pose
From a plank, slide one knee forward toward the opposite wrist. Shin diagonal (or parallel). Lower your torso over the front leg. 2 min each side. Deep glute and hip external rotation.
Form tip: flex the front foot to protect the knee. Place a folded towel under the hip if it floats off the ground
Hamstring stretch (progressive)
Weeks 1–4: seated forward fold reaching toward feet. Weeks 5–8: prop one heel on a step, lean forward. Weeks 9–12: active straight-leg stretch. 90 sec each side throughout.
Active split hold
Weeks 1–2: high lunge with hands on floor. Weeks 3–5: lower hips, hands still down. Weeks 6–9: hips hovering low. Weeks 10–12: full extension attempt. Hold 60–90 sec each side — breathe and relax into it.
Form tip: keep hips square for front splits — don't let the back hip rotate open
Butterfly stretch
Seated, soles of feet together. Hold feet, let knees fall open. 60 sec. Can press elbows into inner thighs to increase. Targets adductors for middle splits progress.
Frog pose
On all fours, walk knees as wide as comfortable. Let hips sink toward the floor. 90 sec. The deepest adductor stretch — the final thing to open before middle splits. Go slow and breathe.

General mobility (Saturday after beach) 🌀

90-90 hip switch
Sit with both legs bent at 90 degrees — one in front, one to the side. Rotate between internal and external hip rotation. 10 slow reps each side. The best all-round hip mobility drill available.
World's greatest stretch
From a lunge, plant same-side hand inside the front foot. Reach the other arm to the sky, rotating your torso open. Then bring that arm down and thread it under your body. 5 reps each side. Hits hip flexor, thoracic spine, and hamstring simultaneously.
Thoracic spine rotations
On all fours, one hand behind your head. Rotate that elbow up toward the ceiling, following with your eyes. 10 each side. Counteracts the forward-hunching posture from desk work and running.
Deep squat hold
Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out. Squat as deep as you can and hold 60–90 sec. Use a doorframe or post for balance if needed. Opens ankles, hips, and groin simultaneously.
