🌈 Your cycle & training

🔴 Menstrual phase — Days 1–5
Energy and strength are at their lowest. Oestrogen and progesterone are both low. This is not a phase to push — it is a phase to honour.

Training: Keep runs easy and conversational. Skip or reduce strength if cramping. Yoga, walking, and gentle stretching are ideal. Your pain tolerance is also lower, so don't be hard on yourself if everything feels harder.
Flexibility: Actually a great time for gentle stretching — the body is more relaxed.
Nutrition: Iron-rich foods (red meat, lentils, dark leafy greens). Magnesium for cramps. Stay warm. More sleep.
🌿 Follicular phase — Days 6–13
Rising oestrogen means rising energy, mood, and motivation. This is your feel-good, do-everything phase. Your body responds best to new challenges here.

Training: Best phase for tempo runs, interval sessions, and heavy strength work. Your body recovers faster now. Try your hardest sessions in this window. Sand sprints on Saturday will feel electric.
Flexibility: Muscles are more pliable — great for splits progress.
Nutrition: Higher carb days support the energy you have. Cruciferous veg (broccoli, kale) help oestrogen metabolism.
☀️ Ovulation — Days 14–17
Peak oestrogen and a testosterone surge. You are strongest, fastest, and most coordinated right now. This is your superpower window.

Training: Go for it. Long runs, PB attempts on pace, heavy lifts. Your grip strength, power output, and pain tolerance are all at their highest. If a big RunKeeper session falls here, race it.
Caution: Oestrogen also loosens ligaments slightly around ovulation — warm up properly and don't do anything reckless with your joints.
Nutrition: Anti-inflammatory foods. Fibre-rich to support oestrogen clearance.
🌙 Luteal phase — Days 18–35
Progesterone rises, temperature increases slightly, and energy gradually drops toward period. Early luteal (days 18–24) is still strong. Late luteal (days 25+) is where PMS symptoms may appear.

Training: Shift toward steady, moderate effort. Relaxed runs feel better than intervals. Strength work is still effective but you may need longer rest between sets. Listen to your body — fatigue and mood changes are hormonal, not weakness.
Flexibility: Progesterone can cause some water retention — don't be discouraged if splits feel tighter.
Nutrition: Complex carbs reduce cravings. Magnesium and B6 for mood. More protein for satiety. Reduce salt to minimise bloating.

Pre-run warm-up (5 min)

Always dynamic before a run — never static holds. Gets blood into the joints before impact.
Leg swings
Stand next to a wall. 10 swings forward/back each leg, then 10 side to side. Swing from the hip socket, not the knee.
Form tip: keep your planted leg soft (not locked), core lightly engaged throughout
Hip circles
Hands on hips, draw big slow circles with your hips. 10 each direction per leg. Think hula hoop — really exaggerate the movement.
Form tip: initiate the circle from deep in the hip socket, not just moving your knees
Walking lunges with a twist
10 walking lunge steps. As you lunge, rotate your torso toward your front knee. Alternate sides. Opens hips and fires up core simultaneously.
Form tip: front knee stays directly over your ankle
High knees (slow and deliberate)
20 reps, lifting each knee toward hip height. Activation not cardio — slow and controlled.
Form tip: drive through the ball of your standing foot on each rep, not the heel
Ankle circles + calf raises
10 full slow circles each ankle, then 15 calf raises both feet. Preps the achilles and ankle joint for impact.
Glute kickbacks
15 each side standing (hands on wall) or on all fours. Squeeze the glute hard at the top.
Form tip: keep hips square — don't let them rotate open as you kick

Post-run stretch (10–15 min)

Do this within 10 minutes of finishing while still warm. On Wed and Sat, go straight into splits work right after.
Standing quad stretch
Hold ankle, pull heel toward glute, stand tall. 45 sec each side. Keep your hips level — don't let the stretched side hike up.
Form tip: squeeze the glute of the stretched leg and tuck the pelvis slightly
Standing calf stretch
Hands on a wall, straight leg back with heel flat. 45 sec. Then bend that back knee 30 sec for the soleus (deeper calf). Each side.
Lying hamstring stretch
On your back, loop a towel around one foot. Gently straighten leg toward the sky. Other leg stays flat. 60 sec each side.
Form tip: don't force the leg straight — let it find its range
Hip flexor lunge stretch
Low lunge, back knee on ground. Sink hips forward and down. 60 sec each side. Most important stretch for runners — tight hip flexors cause lower back pain and shorten stride.
Form tip: tuck the tailbone under to target the hip flexor rather than just the quad
Figure-4 glute stretch
Lying on back, cross one ankle over opposite knee. Pull uncrossed leg toward chest. 60 sec each side. Targets the piriformis which gets tight in runners.
Child's pose
Knees wide, sit hips toward heels, arms extended forward. 90 sec. Decompresses the spine after running.

Core circuit A — runner stability (Tuesday)

Dead bug
Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90 degrees. Slowly lower opposite arm and leg toward the floor, return. 3 x 10 each side. Lower back MUST stay flat — if it lifts, you have gone too far.
Form tip: exhale as you lower the limbs. Breath braces the core and keeps the back down
Hollow body hold
Lie on back, press lower back flat, lift arms overhead and legs to about 45 degrees. Hold 25–45 sec (increases each week). Banana shape. Builds the deep core that holds your posture in the last 2km of a race.
Form tip: if your lower back lifts, raise your legs higher until it stays flat
Plank with shoulder taps
In a strong plank, tap opposite shoulder alternately. 3 x 20 taps. Hips stay perfectly still. Anti-rotation core work — exactly what running demands.
Form tip: widen your feet for stability. Zero hip sway allowed
Side plank
On forearm, body in one straight diagonal line, hips lifted. 3 x 25–45 sec each side (increases each week). Builds lateral hip stability that protects your knees with every stride.
Form tip: don't let the bottom hip sag. Stack your feet or stagger them for stability
Glute bridge march
In a glute bridge (hips raised), lift one foot slightly, hold 2 sec, switch. 3 x 10 each leg. Challenges glute and core to stabilise together — exactly what happens in each stride.

Core circuit B — strength and toning (Thu + Sat)

Bicycle crunches
Hands behind head, rotate elbow to opposite knee while extending the other leg. 3–4 x 16–24 SLOW reps. The slower you go, the harder it is — that is the whole point.
Form tip: rotation from your ribcage, not your neck. Lower back stays pressed to floor
Leg raises
Lying on back, legs straight or slightly bent. Lower from 90 degrees slowly to just above the floor. 3–4 x 12–18. The lowering phase is the work — go as slow as you can.
Form tip: if back arches, bend your knees more or raise the stopping point
Russian twists
Seated, lean back 45 degrees, rotate side to side touching the ground each time. 3–4 x 20–28. Hold a water bottle for load. Chest stays tall throughout.
Mountain climbers
In a solid plank, drive knees to chest alternately. 3–4 x 30–45 sec. Don't let the hips rise. Core endurance and cardio in one.
Form tip: find the pace where you can still breathe through your nose — that's your working pace
Superman hold
Face down, arms extended overhead. Lift arms, chest and legs simultaneously. Hold 2 sec at top, lower slowly. 3 x 12. Lower back strength — the forgotten part of core training.
Plank hold
Body in one straight line head to heels. 3 x 45–60 sec. Squeeze everything — glutes, core, quads.
Form tip: don't let hips drop or pike up. Imagine a rod running from head to heels
Toe touches
Legs straight up, reach fingertips toward toes. 3 x 18–20. Full crunch at the top, squeeze hard.
Form tip: lift your shoulder blades completely off the floor at the top

Upper body (Thursday)

Push-up (follow your progression)
3 sets at current level from the push-up tracker. Rest 60–90 sec between sets. Every variation counts.
Form tip: hands wider than shoulders, body completely straight, elbows track back at 45 degrees — not flared sideways
Resistance band rows
Anchor band at chest height. Pull both elbows back simultaneously, squeezing shoulder blades. 3 x 15. Builds the back strength that makes push-ups easier.
Form tip: shoulders stay DOWN away from ears throughout — no shrugging
Band pull-aparts
Hold band at chest height, arms extended. Pull apart to a T shape, squeezing shoulder blades. 3 x 20. Great for posture and rear shoulder strength.
Pike push-ups
Hips high in a V shape (downward dog). Bend elbows to lower top of head toward floor, push back up. 3 x 8–10. Heavy shoulder targeting. Bend knees to make easier.
Tricep dips
Hands on a bench or low wall behind you. Lower by bending elbows to 90 degrees, press back up. 3 x 10. Critical for the push-up lockout at the top.
Form tip: elbows point straight BACK, not out to the sides

Strength A — lower body (Tuesday)

Single-leg glute bridges
Lie on back, one foot flat on floor, other leg up. Drive through planted heel to lift hips. 3 x 15 each side. Most important exercise for preventing running knee injuries.
Form tip: squeeze the glute hard at the top for 1 second. Hips stay level — not twisted
Reverse lunges
Step one foot back, lower back knee toward floor, push off front foot to return. 3 x 12 each leg. Safer for knees than forward lunges. Add weight with rocks or a backpack.
Form tip: front knee stays over your ankle — if past your toes, widen your stance
Lateral band walks
Band around ankles, walk sideways 20 steps each direction. Slight squat throughout. Hip abductors keep knees tracking straight when running — this is their direct training.
Form tip: feet never fully together — keep constant tension on the band
Single-leg calf raises
Stand on one foot, lower heel below a step edge, rise up on ball of foot. 3 x 20 each side. Builds achilles tendon resilience — critical as running volume increases.
Form tip: the slow lowering (eccentric) phase is where most adaptation happens
Step-ups
Step one foot onto a kerb, rock, doorstep, or any low surface (15–20cm is plenty). Drive through that heel to lift your body up. 3 x 12 each leg.

No surface? Do slow single-leg squats instead — stand on one leg, bend to 60 degrees, pause, stand. Same muscles, same benefit.

Strength B — beach session (Saturday)

Sand sprints (15–20m)
6–8 reps. Flat out, fully recover between each (1–2 min walk back). Sand increases resistance and reduces joint impact. Barefoot on firm sand is ideal.
Form tip: lean from ankles not waist, drive knees high, pump arms hard and fast
Jump squats
Squat to 90 degrees, explode upward. Land softly with bent knees — absorb the impact. 3 x 12. Sand makes landings more forgiving.
Push-up variation (extra practice)
2 sets at current level. A second weekly push-up session accelerates progress faster than doing more sets in one day.
Bear crawls
Knees hovering low above sand, crawl forward 20m. Keep hips DOWN — resist sticking bum up. Full body: core, shoulders, hips, coordination. Sand makes this dramatically harder.
Resistance band rows (anchored)
Anchor to a post or tree. 3 x 15 same technique as Thursday. Second back strength session of the week.

Splits routine (20 min) — warm muscles only

Always after running or a warm-up. Cold stretching does almost nothing. Warm muscle is pliable muscle.
Low lunge hold
Step into a lunge, back knee on ground. Sink hips forward and down. 2 min each side. Primary exercise for front splits.
Form tip: tuck your tailbone under and gently push the back hip toward the floor
Pigeon pose
From a plank, slide one knee forward toward opposite wrist. Lower torso over front leg. 2 min each side. Deep glute and hip external rotation.
Form tip: flex front foot to protect knee. Place towel under hip if it floats off ground
Hamstring stretch (progressive)
Weeks 1–4: seated forward fold. Weeks 5–8: heel on step. Weeks 9–12: active straight-leg stretch. 90 sec each side.
Active split hold
Weeks 1–2: high lunge hands on floor. Weeks 3–5: hips lower. Weeks 6–9: hovering low. Weeks 10–12: full extension attempt. 60–90 sec each side.
Form tip: keep hips square — don't let the back hip rotate open
Butterfly stretch
Seated, soles of feet together. Let knees fall open. 60 sec. Press elbows into inner thighs to increase. Targets adductors for middle splits.
Frog pose
On all fours, walk knees as wide as comfortable. Hips sink toward floor. 90 sec. Deepest adductor stretch — last thing to open before middle splits.

General mobility (Saturday after beach)

90-90 hip switch
Sit with both legs at 90 degrees — one in front, one to side. Rotate between internal and external hip rotation. 10 slow reps each side. Best all-round hip mobility drill.
World's greatest stretch
From a lunge, plant same-side hand inside front foot. Reach other arm to sky rotating torso open. Then thread that arm under body. 5 reps each side. Hits hip flexor, thoracic spine, and hamstring simultaneously.
Thoracic spine rotations
On all fours, one hand behind head. Rotate that elbow up toward ceiling. 10 each side. Counteracts the forward-hunching from desk work and running.
Deep squat hold
Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out. Squat as deep as possible, hold 60–90 sec. Use a doorframe for balance if needed. Opens ankles, hips, and groin simultaneously.
